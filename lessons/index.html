Why school holiday lessons?

I want to give the best instruction I can, and I believe that taking a three-week break from violin lessons every 2½ months presents a major barrier to progress. Many students simply don’t practise over the break, which I believe is (at least in part) because students quickly lose sight of their learning objectives without the guidance of their teacher.

Actually, the school holidays the absolute best time for your child to get lots of wonderful, joyful violin playing done, and continuing lessons between school terms is an extremely powerful antidote for “holiday decay”. The three-week break between the last and first lessons of a school term can very easily undo months of effort, especially the work of establishing a steady routine.

Of course, a break is needed from time to time (even from something as enjoyable as violin practice!), and many take the holidays for family trips. I’m flexible, and we can move the lessons around as needed.
