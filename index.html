Lesson packages

For all lessons, I will come to your place of residence! This is more convenient for you - no hassle of traffic, parking or bad weather.

Junior - $660
The school holidays are the absolute best time for your child to get lots of wonderful violin practice done, and continuing lessons between school terms is a powerful antidote for “holiday decay”!

If your child resists playing the violin between school terms, it may be because children are liable to quickly lose sight of their goals in the absence of a teacher’s guidance. The three-week break between the last and first lessons of a school term can very easily undo months of effort, especially the work of establishing a steady routine.

With this package, you will receive two extra lessons for the price of one, when compared with the Basic package.

Basic Junior - $600
Ten thirty-minute lessons. This package does not include lessons during the school holidays. For some, the break between terms is an important time for the family to travel together. If it’s not convenient for you to continue lessons through the break, this package is for you.

Intermediate - $840
Students who have been playing for a few years may benefit from a longer lesson.

I recommend moving up to 45-minute lesson for students who:
  • Have been playing for two or three years
  • Aged 10 and up
  • Are playing repertoire at an AMEB grade 1-3 level
  • Demonstrate patience, curiosity, initiative, and diligence
  • Adult beginners
That being said, any student may benefit from a longer lesson. For many students, it may take 10-15 minutes just for the body to warm up and the mind to calm down. A 45-minute lesson allows a little more time to settle in at the start, so that the real meat of the lesson can be that much deeper. It’s just like adding some deep stretches before a big workout.

Basic Intermediate - $760
Ten 45-minute lessons.

Advanced - $1200
Twelve one-hour lessons. An hour allows for much deeper time to be spent on practice techniques, sight-reading, duets, technical work, and even aural skills and music theory. One-hour lessons are best for students who are most serious about the violin. The challenges of violin at this more advanced stage are nuanced and take some time to tease out. An hour-long lesson is necessary for students who want to play at an AMEB grade 5 level or higher, for secondary students preparing for their ATAR, and for those who want to pursue violin at a tertiary level.

Again, any student is likely to benefit from an hour-long lesson. An hour-long lesson may be preferable to a 45-minute lesson, since it allows a little time in the middle for a short 5-minute focus break.
